## Idea 1 (HIGHEST PRIORITY): Foil-2 DSDF Magnitude Augmentation for Tandem Wake Robustness

**What it is.** During training, for tandem samples, randomly scale the foil-2 signed-distance-function (sADF / secondary dsdf) channels (x indices 2–9 are DSDF gradient channels; foil-2 sADF is in indices 6–9 per `prepare_multi.py`) by a factor drawn from U[0.8, 1.2]. This is a direct domain-randomization on the foil-2 DSDF, not the gap/stagger scalar. The idea is that val_tandem_transfer uses NACA6416 as the front foil, which has a very different camber and thickness distribution than the NACA2412 and NACA9412 in training. The sADF field encodes the foil surface geometry implicitly. By perturbing this during training, we force the model to be less reliant on memorizing the exact sADF pattern for seen foil shapes — exactly what the transfer test probes.

**Why it might help.** Gap/stagger aug (#2115, merged, p_oodc -4.9%) proved that domain randomization on scalar tandem descriptors works. The sADF channels are the *geometric* analog of gap/stagger: they are the per-node encoding of how far the foil surface is and in which direction. The NACA6416 transfer failure is geometrically different from the NACA2412/9412 training shapes, so randomizing the sADF representation should improve generalization over unseen foil shapes. Crucially, this targets p_tan directly: val_tandem_transfer is the geometric transfer test, not a scalar OOD test.

**Key concern / failure mode.** If the sADF gradient directions are perturbed (as opposed to just scale), the model could learn incorrect surface normals. A scalar scaling preserves direction but adjusts magnitude. Limit perturbation to multiplicative scale only, applied uniformly per sample (not per-node), to avoid sign flips or directional corruption.

**Implementation.**
- In the augmentation block (around line 1542), immediately after the gap/stagger aug block, add:
  ```python
  if cfg.aug_dsdf2_sigma > 0.0:
      _is_tandem_aug = (x[:, 0, 22].abs() > 0.01)  # tandem detection
      if _is_tandem_aug.any():
          # Multiplicative scale per sample, applied to foil-2 dsdf channels (indices 6-9 in raw x)
          # Before x standardization, raw_dsdf = x[:, :, 2:10], foil-2 is [6:10] = x[:, :, 8:12]
          # Actually foil-2 dsdf is at raw x indices 6-9 (0-indexed per prepare_multi.py)
          _scale = torch.exp(torch.randn(x.size(0), device=x.device) * cfg.aug_dsdf2_sigma)
          _scale = _scale * _is_tandem_aug.float() + (~_is_tandem_aug).float()  # identity for non-tandem
          x[:, :, 6:10] = x[:, :, 6:10] * _scale.view(-1, 1, 1)  # foil-2 dsdf columns
  ```
  Note: this must be applied BEFORE `x = (x - stats["x_mean"]) / stats["x_std"]` (line 1568).
- Add `aug_dsdf2_sigma: float = 0.0` to Config.
- Sweep: sigma=0.05 (log-scale), 0.10, 0.15. Start with 0.10 as the primary value.
- No change to any other component; orthogonal to the shared srf_head.

**Exact indices to verify.** The student must confirm which columns of x correspond to foil-2 dsdf by reading `data/prepare_multi.py` — the `raw_dsdf = x[:, :, 2:10]` comment in line 1556 tells us the raw DSDF runs from index 2 to 9, but whether indices 2–5 are foil-1 dsdf and 6–9 are foil-2 dsdf must be verified from the preprocessing code. If the foil-2 dsdf channels differ, adjust accordingly.

**Confidence:** Strong — direct extension of the domain-randomization principle that already proved effective (#2115). The mechanism is well-targeted at val_tandem_transfer's specific failure mode (unseen foil geometry). Risk: wrong channel indices would corrupt training.

## Idea 2: Tandem Sample Mixup in DSDF Space (Foil-Shape Interpolation)

**What it is.** For tandem training samples, perform mixup between two tandem samples in the DSDF channel space only (not positions or targets). Concretely: given two tandem samples A and B, form a new training sample with x_mixed = lambda*x_A + (1-lambda)*x_B for the DSDF features only (foil-1 sdf in indices 2–5, foil-2 sdf in indices 6–9), while keeping sample A's positions and targets unchanged. Lambda ~ Beta(0.4, 0.4). This interpolates between different foil shape encodings, synthetically creating "intermediate" foil shapes the model must learn to predict on. It biases the model toward smooth interpolation in shape space, which is precisely the generalization we need for unseen NACA6416.

**Why it might help.** Mixup has a well-established theory: it enforces linear interpolation consistency (Vicinal Risk Minimization). Standard mixup (#2xxx — tried globally) did not help, but that mixed everything including positions and targets, which is physically incorrect. DSDF-only mixup is physically motivated: the DSDF channels encode the foil shape implicitly, and real unseen foil shapes do lie in a smooth space between seen ones. NACA6416 (camber 6, max-camber-pos 4, thickness 16%) lies geometrically between NACA2412 and NACA9412 in some sense. DSDF interpolation creates synthetic in-between geometries during training without corrupting the spatial structure.

**Key concern / failure mode.** The DSDF gradient directions at a given node are unit-vector-like: they point toward the nearest surface. Mixing two samples at the same spatial location but with different foil shapes means the mixed DSDF at node X points "between" two surface directions — physically meaningful only if the foil shapes are similar. Far from the foil, both shapes have similar DSDF directions; near the foil, they can differ significantly. High lambda (close to 1.0) is safer. Use Beta(0.7, 0.4) to bias lambda toward 1.0 (mostly sample A, small admixture of B).

**Implementation.**
- After gap/stagger aug block, add tandem-only DSDF mixup:
  - Sample `lambda ~ Beta(0.7, 0.4)` per tandem sample pair.
  - Identify tandem samples in the batch; pair them randomly.
  - Mix only feature indices 2–9 (all DSDF channels) in x.
  - Targets and positions unchanged.
  - Only activate after epoch 20 (let model stabilize first): `aug_start_epoch=20`.
- Add flag `aug_dsdf_mixup: bool = False`.
- This requires batch size >= 2 for tandem samples; with batch_size=4 and balanced sampler, expect ~1.3 tandem samples per batch on average (3 domains, B=4 → ~1.3 tandem). May need to skip when fewer than 2 tandem samples in batch.

**Confidence:** Moderate — theoretically well-motivated, but the DSDF mixing may not produce geometrically coherent interpolations, especially near the foil surface. Lower confidence than Idea 1.

## Idea 3: Aft-Foil SRF with KNN Volume Context (Context-Aware AftSRF)

**What it is.** The current `aft_srf_head` (AftFoilRefinementHead, `--aft_foil_srf`, merged #2104) takes only the hidden representation and base prediction of boundary-ID-7 surface nodes. The `SurfaceRefinementContextHead` (already implemented but not default) also accepts K-nearest volume neighbor context. Extend the aft-foil SRF head to use the same neighborhood context: for each foil-2 surface node, gather the hidden representations of its K=8 nearest volume neighbors in the foil-2 refinement zone (zone_id=2), average them, and concatenate to the surface node's hidden state before the correction MLP.

**Why it might help.** The aft-foil operates in the wake of the fore-foil. The pressure at the aft-foil surface is strongly influenced by the flow field immediately upstream — in the volume nodes just upstream of foil-2. The current `aft_srf_head` only sees the surface node's own hidden representation, which may not have adequately captured the upstream wake structure. Giving it explicit access to the nearest-neighbor volume representations (particularly those in zone_id=2, the foil-2 refinement zone) provides direct geometric context about the wake state hitting the aft foil. This is an architectural improvement with zero change to training procedure.

**Why it's different from SurfaceRefinementContextHead.** The existing context head operates on all surface nodes globally. The proposed change applies KNN context only to aft-foil surface nodes, and specifically restricts the KNN search to volume nodes in the aft-foil refinement zone. This is more targeted and preserves the foil-1 SRF behavior.

**Implementation.**
- Modify `AftFoilRefinementHead` to accept an optional `vol_hidden` context tensor.
- In the forward pass for aft_srf_head (lines 1702–1715), after extracting `aft_idx`:
  - Use positions `x[:, :, :2]` and the `_aft_foil_mask` to find K=8 nearest volume neighbors for each aft-foil node.
  - Use zone_id information (available as feature in x) to restrict KNN to zone_id=2 volume nodes.
  - Average the hidden representations of the K neighbors, concatenate with `aft_hidden`.
  - Adjust `in_dim` of AftFoilRefinementHead from `n_hidden * 2 + out_dim` to `n_hidden * 3 + out_dim`.
- Add flag `aft_foil_srf_context: bool = False`.
- Warning: KNN computation adds runtime cost; precompute or batch carefully.

**Confidence:** Moderate-high — the mechanism is physically well-motivated (wake context) and the SRF context approach has worked for the shared head. Key unknown: whether the hidden representation contains sufficient wake-state information to make the volume context meaningful at this stage.

## Idea 4: NACA Shape Embedding in Input Features (Learnable Per-Foil-Family Shift)

**What it is.** The input feature vector already contains foil-2 NACA code as a scalar or set of scalars (indices in the 24-dim vector from `prepare_multi.py`). Instead of relying on the raw NACA parameter value, learn a small embedding: map each unique NACA family identifier (e.g. "2412", "9412", "6416") to a trainable 4-dim embedding vector, add it to the foil-2 DSDF channel representation as an additive bias. At val_tandem_transfer time, the NACA6416 foil is OOD — but its 4-parameter NACA code (camber, camber-position, thickness: 6, 4, 16) can be mapped to a new embedding via interpolation from the training NACA codes (2412, 9412).

**Why it might help.** The fundamental difficulty with val_tandem_transfer is that NACA6416 has never appeared as a front foil in tandem training. The DSDF field encodes its geometry implicitly, but the model has only learned to use DSDF patterns from NACA2412 and NACA9412. An explicit foil-family embedding would give the model a higher-level abstraction: "this is a high-camber foil" vs "this is a thin foil" vs "this is an asymmetric foil." At inference on NACA6416, the embedding can be derived from its NACA code parameters directly (no lookup table needed — compute from parameters).

**Key concern / failure mode.** With only 2 training foil families (NACA2412, NACA9412), a 4-dim embedding will overfit to these two data points. The embedding would not generalize to NACA6416 at inference unless the embedding space is explicitly regularized or tied to NACA parameters. This limits the approach — it is only useful if the embedding is computed from NACA parameters (camber, camber-pos, thickness) rather than as a free lookup. This is technically an input feature engineering change, not a learnable embedding: map (c, p, t) → 4-dim via a small MLP trained from scratch.

**Implementation.** Requires knowing which indices in x encode the foil-2 NACA parameters. From `prepare_multi.py` (read-only), foil-2 NACA code is added to the input. The student would need to inspect this file to determine exact indices, then add a small `nn.Linear(3, 4)` mapping from (camber, camber_pos, thickness) to a 4-dim bias, concatenate to the foil-2 DSDF features before input to the network.

**Confidence:** Low-moderate. Strong theoretical motivation but very few training data points for the embedding. More likely to help than hurt if implemented as a direct NACA parameter → additive feature mapping (not a lookup table), but high risk of the embedding not learning useful structure with only 2 training family examples.

## Idea 5: Pressure-Gradient Consistency Auxiliary Loss (Soft Physics)

**What it is.** For surface nodes on the aft foil, add an auxiliary loss that penalizes large pressure gradient discontinuities between adjacent surface nodes. Concretely: sort foil-2 surface nodes by their arc-length (x-coordinate approximation along chord), compute first-order finite differences of predicted pressure between adjacent nodes, and penalize with L1 loss when the gradient differs strongly from the ground-truth gradient. This is a weak form of the smoothness constraint without needing to solve the Poisson equation — it just checks that the predicted pressure gradient field along the foil surface is consistent with ground truth.

**Why it might help.** The p_tan metric is surface MAE on the tandem transfer case. Qualitatively, errors on val_tandem_transfer may be "spiky" — incorrect local pressure peaks or troughs at individual surface nodes — rather than a global offset. The current loss treats each surface node independently (L1 per-node). A gradient consistency loss would penalize local oscillations that are present in the prediction but absent in the target, or vice versa. This is analogous to the TV (Total Variation) regularization used in image restoration, applied to the pressure profile along the foil surface.

**Key concern / failure mode.** The node ordering for gradient computation requires arc-length sorting, which is non-trivial on an irregular mesh. A simple x-coordinate sort is approximate but may suffice. More critically, for tandem cases, the aft foil has a different chord-aligned coordinate system than the fore foil — the sort needs to be foil-local, not global. The `_aft_foil_mask` already identifies foil-2 surface nodes, so this is feasible. Loss weight must be small (0.01–0.05 of the main surface loss) to avoid over-smoothing real pressure gradients.

**Implementation.**
- After computing `surf_loss`, add:
  ```python
  if cfg.surf_grad_consistency and is_tandem_batch.any():
      # For tandem aft-foil nodes only
      aft_mask = _aft_foil_mask  # [B, N] already computed
      for b in range(B):
          if not is_tandem_batch[b]: continue
          aft_nodes = aft_mask[b].nonzero().squeeze(-1)  # node indices
          if aft_nodes.numel() < 3: continue
          # Sort by x-coordinate for approximate arc-length ordering
          x_coords = x[b, aft_nodes, 0]
          sort_order = x_coords.argsort()
          aft_sorted = aft_nodes[sort_order]
          pred_p = pred[b, aft_sorted, 2]   # predicted pressure (normalized)
          true_p = y_norm[b, aft_sorted, 2]  # true pressure (normalized)
          pred_grad = pred_p[1:] - pred_p[:-1]
          true_grad = true_p[1:] - true_p[:-1]
          grad_loss += (pred_grad - true_grad).abs().mean()
      loss = loss + cfg.surf_grad_weight * grad_loss / B
  ```
- Add `surf_grad_consistency: bool = False`, `surf_grad_weight: float = 0.05`.

**Confidence:** Moderate — TV/gradient regularization is well-established in PDE-constrained problems. The physical motivation is sound. The main risk is that approximate arc-length ordering introduces noise that degrades rather than helps the gradient loss. Start with a very small weight (0.01) and verify the gradient norms.

## Idea 6: Tandem-Aware Temperature Annealing for Transolver Slices

**What it is.** The Transolver attention uses a learned temperature parameter (`self.temperature`) that controls how sharply nodes are assigned to slices. A lower temperature produces harder, more specialized slice assignments; a higher temperature produces softer, more distributed assignments. Currently this temperature is shared across all samples. Hypothesis: tandem samples benefit from softer slice assignments (more distributed attention over the full domain, to capture long-range fore-aft foil interactions), while single-foil samples benefit from sharper assignments (strong localization of boundary-layer effects). Implement a trainable per-regime temperature offset: `temp_tandem = temperature + tandem_offset`, where `tandem_offset` is initialized to a small positive value.

**Why it might help.** The tandem flow physics is fundamentally non-local: the pressure on the aft foil depends on the wake of the fore foil, which is physically distant. Sharper slice assignments may be forcing the model to use local slice tokens that cannot capture this non-local dependency. The `tandem_temp_offset` parameter is already defined in the `Physics_Attention_Irregular_Mesh` class (seen in line 750 context), but it is unclear whether it is currently being used. Check if `tandem_temp_offset` is actively connected; if not, activating it via a flag is a minimal change.

**Implementation.**
- Check if `tandem_temp_offset` in `Physics_Attention_Irregular_Mesh` is already used in the attention forward pass. If not, enable it: pass `tandem_mask` (already computed as `is_tandem` in line 836) to the attention computation, and add `temperature + tandem_temp_offset * tandem_mask` instead of bare `temperature`.
- The `is_tandem` scalar is already computed and passed as `tandem_mask=is_tandem` to each block (line 843). It just needs to be wired into the temperature computation inside `Physics_Attention_Irregular_Mesh`.
- Initialize `tandem_temp_offset` to +0.1 (softer for tandem) and let it learn.
- Add `tandem_temp_anneal: bool = False` flag to Config.

**Confidence:** Low-moderate. The mechanism is theoretically motivated, and `tandem_temp_offset` is already defined in the code — suggesting this was planned but perhaps not fully wired up. If the parameter exists but is not connected, this is a very low-risk change to complete the intended design. If it is already connected (and thus already in use), this idea is moot.

## Idea 7 (BOLD): Geometric Data Augmentation via Foil-2 AoA Rotation (Tandem-Specific)

**What it is.** The current `aug=aoa_perturb` rotates the entire mesh by a small angle, including the velocity field. For tandem samples, the fore-foil and aft-foil have independent AoAs. Implement a foil-2-specific AoA perturbation: rotate only the aft-foil surface nodes (boundary ID=7, identified by `_aft_foil_mask`) and the foil-2 refinement zone nodes (zone_id=2) by a small independent angle delta_aoa2, while leaving the fore-foil nodes and single-foil cases unchanged. The rotation applies to both the (x,y) position and DSDF gradient features for those nodes, and to the velocity targets for those nodes.

**Why it might help.** val_tandem_transfer tests an unseen front foil shape (NACA6416), but the aft foil shape in that evaluation may also be at AoAs not well represented in training. By augmenting aft-foil AoA independently from fore-foil AoA during training, we increase the effective diversity of (fore_AoA, aft_AoA) combinations seen during training. Currently, training tandem cases have correlated fore/aft AoAs; independent perturbation breaks this correlation. The aerodynamic reality is that fore/aft AoAs CAN be set independently in real tandem systems.

**Key concern / failure mode.** Rotating just the aft-foil nodes while keeping fore-foil nodes fixed creates a geometric inconsistency at the interface between the two refinement zones. This could confuse the model by producing configurations with "impossible" inter-foil geometry. The perturbation must be small (|delta_aoa2| < 0.5 degrees, ~0.009 rad) to minimize interface discontinuities, and should only be applied after the model is well-converged (aug_start_epoch >= 50).

**Implementation.**
- Add `aug_aft_aoa_sigma: float = 0.0` to Config.
- In the augmentation block, after gap/stagger aug, for tandem samples:
  - Sample `delta_aoa2 ~ N(0, cfg.aug_aft_aoa_sigma)` per sample.
  - For nodes in `_aft_foil_mask` (boundary ID=7) only:
    - Rotate x[:, aft_nodes, :2] (positions) by delta_aoa2.
    - Rotate DSDF gradient pairs at aft nodes: indices (2,3), (4,5), (6,7), (8,9).
    - Rotate velocity targets y[:, aft_nodes, :2] by delta_aoa2.
- Start with sigma=0.003 rad (~0.17 degrees) and validate before increasing.
- This requires knowing `_aft_foil_mask` before x standardization — needs reorganization of the `_aft_foil_mask` computation to be BEFORE the augmentation block.

**Confidence:** Moderate — physically motivated and directly targets the tandem-specific generalization gap. Implementation complexity is non-trivial (requires reorganizing the mask computation order in train.py). The interface discontinuity risk is real but manageable with small perturbations.
